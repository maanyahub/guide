Welcome to the Holy City of Varanasi!

Dear Maanya's Mom, here are some amazing places and delicious dishes you must explore in the timeless city of Varanasi.

Places to Visit

  • Chet Singh Ghat: 6 km from Varanasi Junction, a relatively peaceful and less crowded ghat.
  • Ramnagar Fort: Reach by boat, crossing the Ganga. Also features a museum.
  • Chunar Fort: 1 hour from Varanasi Junction.
  • Sampurnanand Sanskrit University: A treasure of knowledge, a must-visit.
  • Lamhi Village: Birthplace of Munshi Premchand, offers a unique vibe.
  • Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple: A must-visit. Whatever we pray for here gets fulfilled.
  • Markandeya Mahadev Temple: Another spiritual place to visit.
  • Pashupatinath Temple: The other version (branch) of the original Pashupatinath Temple in Nepal.
  • Dashashwamedh Ghat: Famous for Ganga Aarti.
  • Assi Ghat: A vibrant ghat where locals and tourists meet.
  • Manikarnika Ghat: One of the holiest cremation grounds.
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple: One of the most famous and revered temples in India.
  • Durga Temple: Known for its striking red color.
  • Tulsi Manas Temple: Dedicated to Lord Rama, built at the location where Tulsidas wrote Ramcharitmanas.
  • Bharat Mata Temple: A unique temple with a map of India carved in marble.
  • Alamgir Mosque: Overlooking the Ganga, blending Hindu and Islamic architecture.
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU): One of India's largest and most prestigious universities.
  • New Vishwanath Temple: Inside the BHU campus, this is a replica of the original Kashi Vishwanath Temple.
  • Sarnath: A Buddhist pilgrimage site where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon.
  • Bhonsle Ghat: Known for its beautiful architecture and historical significance.
  • Nepali Temple: A replica of the famous Pashupatinath Temple in Kathmandu.

Dishes to Try

  • Dahi Chena Vada
  • Malaiyo: Shiva's prasadam, only available in Varanasi, prepared while keeping it under moonlight.
  • Kachori Sabji
  • Tamatar Chat
  • Choora Matar
  • Launglata
  • Chana Chaat
  • Banarasi Paan
  • Kulhad Banarasi Chai
  • Baati Chokha (a must-try dish)
  • Jalebi: Served hot with a side of Rabri.
  • Thandai: A traditional drink often infused with spices and almonds.
  • Lassi: Thick, creamy, and best enjoyed with a dollop of malai on top.
